Daisuke Sato

The author who brought stories to life

Here's a timeline of Daisuke Satō's life:

  • 1964 - Daisuke Sato is born in Japan.
  • 1990s - Sato begins his career as a manga artist and writer, contributing to various publications and magazines.
  • 2006 - Sato collaborates with artist Shoji Sato to create "Highschool of the Dead," a manga series that combines elements of horror, action, and survival.
  • 2007-2013 - "Highschool of the Dead" is serialized in Fujimi Shobo's Monthly Dragon Age magazine.
  • 2010 - The "Highschool of the Dead" anime adaptation airs, gaining significant popularity both in Japan and internationally.
  • 2011 - Sato and Shoji release the "Highschool of the Dead" OVA (original video animation), further expanding the series' fanbase.
  • 2013 - The manga goes on hiatus following Daisuke Sato's sudden death due to heart disease.
  • 2017 - Despite Sato's passing, "Highschool of the Dead" remains popular among fans, with discussions about a possible continuation or conclusion of the manga continuing to circulate.
  • 2024 - The legacy of "Highschool of the Dead" endures, with the series still fondly remembered by fans and influencing subsequent works in the horror and survival genre.
